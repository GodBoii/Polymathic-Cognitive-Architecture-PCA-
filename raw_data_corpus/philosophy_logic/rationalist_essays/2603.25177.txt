HYPERCONTRACTIVITY TYPE PROPERTY FOR GENERALIZED MEHLER
SEMIGROUPS
LUCIANA ANGIULI AND SIMONE FERRARI∗
Abstract. We investigate the hypercontractivity property of generalized Mehler semigroups on the
Lp-scale with respect to invariant measures. This property is first obtained in the purely theoretical
setting of skew operators and, subsequently, deduced for generalized Mehler semigroups arising
from linear stochastic differential equations perturbed by L´evy noise. When the associated invariant
measure µ lacks a purely Gaussian structure, jump components may prevent the validity of Nelson’s
classical Lp-Lq estimates. However, a summability-improving property can be obtained in the setting
of mixed-norm spaces Xp,q(E; γ, π) related to the factorization of the invariant measure µ = γ∗π into
a Gaussian part γ and an infinitely divisible non-Gaussian part π. As in the classical Gaussian case,
some modified logarithmic Sobolev inequalities with respect to invariant measures can be inferred.

Even for logarithmic Sobolev inequalities, it has been shown in [19] that for Poisson processes

from the Gaussian one, no connection between the modified logarithmic Sobolev inequalities proved
and some hypercontractivity type property has been established.
Thus, naturally, it raises the question of the summability improvement property by the action of a
generalized Mehler semigroup in the non-purely Gaussian case and of the occurrence of some related
logarithmic Sobolev inequalities.
In this paper, we address and give some partial answer to this problem. First, the abstract framework
we work in, is that of skew operators on separable real Banach spaces among which generalized Mehler
semigroups represent a particular case. If E is a real Banach space, we say that T : E →E is a skew
map with respect to a Radon probability measure µ if there exists a probability measure ρ such that
(µ ◦T −1) ∗ρ = µ, see Definition 4.1. We can consider a linear operator PT : Bb(E) →Bb(E) defined

The main contributions of this paper are two hypercontractivity-type estimates for PT , see Theorem
4.10. Such estimates are based on the fact that the invariant measure µ is an appropriate infinitely
divisible measure that can be written as the convolution γ ∗π where γ is a Gaussian measure and
π is an infinitely divisible, non-Gaussian probability measure. This type of decomposition is always
possible if µ is sufficiently regular (see [42, Theorem 4.20]) and allows to consider the mixed-norm
spaces Xp,q(E; γ, π) (see Section 3) that, when p < q are such that

respect to µ but they are given in terms of the integral with respect to π of the entropy with respect
to γ of any smooth enough functions.
