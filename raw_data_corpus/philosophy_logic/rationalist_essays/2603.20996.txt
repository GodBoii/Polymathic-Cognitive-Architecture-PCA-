On Path-dependent Volterra Integral Equations: Strong
Well-posedness and Stochastic Numerics.
Emmanuel Gnabeyeu‚ąó

Abstract
The aim of this paper is to provide a comprehensive analysis of the path-dependent Stochastic
Volterra Integral Equations (SVIEs), in which both the drift and the diffusion coefficients are allowed
to depend on the whole trajectory of the process up to the current time. We investigate the existence
and uniqueness (aka the strong well-posedness) of solutions to such equations in the Lp setting, p > 0,
locally in time and their properties specifically their path regularity and flows. Then, we introduce a
numerical approximation method based on an interpolated K‚ąíintegrated Euler-Maruyama scheme
to simulate numerically the process, and we prove the convergence, with an explicit rate, of this
scheme towards the strong solution in the Lp norm.
Keywords: Path-dependent Stochastic Processes, Volterra Integral Equations, Stochastic Differential
Equations, Integrated Euler Scheme, Strong Convergence Rate, Fractional Kernels.

Introduction
1.1
Literature review
The theory of stochastic Volterra integral equations (SVIEs), seen as a generalization of stochastic
differential equations (SDEs), has its origins in the 1980s and has been widely developed since then.
These equations have garnered significant attention within the mathematical community working on
stochastic dynamical systems, particularly for their applications in population dynamics, biology, and
physics(see e.g. Mohammed (1998)), as they provide a natural framework for modeling non-Markovian
stochastic phenomena.
Their study has also been notably motivated by problems in heat transfer
physics (B. Gripenberg and Saavalainen, 1990), and they have since been the subject of substantial
mathematical investigation. Early studies begin with the seminal and pioneering work of Berger and
Mizel (1980a,b), where the following Volterra integral equation(SVIEs) driven by Brownian motion
with continuous coefficients was introduced:

In the late 1990s, attempts were made within the financial community to incorporate long-memory
effects into continuous-time stochastic volatility models. This paradigm shift has been driven by the
necessity to capture persistent dependencies observed in financial markets, particularly through H-
fractional Brownian motion or Volterra equations with regular K(t, s)(see Coutin and Decreusefond
(2001); Comte and Renault (1998)). In the early 2000s, research shifted to Volterra equations with
singular kernels kernel K(t, s) that blow up as s ‚Üít (i.e. H < 1/2). This transition was fueled by the
development of a new generation of stochastic volatility models, which forgo the Markovian framework
to better align with empirical data. Notably, the study in Gatheral et al. (2018) revealed that volatility
trajectories exhibit very low H¬®older regularity, with H ‚Čą0.1, underscoring the need for non-Markovian
modeling approaches. As a result, there has been a resurgence of interest in SVIEs within mathematical
finance, particularly with the rise of rough volatility models, as highlighted in the work of El Euch and
Rosenbaum (2018); Gnabeyeu et al. (2025); Gnabeyeu (2026). These models, which use the singular
kernel, naturally capture this feature, as their paths have a H¬®older continuity exponent H. Singular
kernel Volterra equations also arise as limiting dynamics in models of order books via nearly unstable
Hawkes processes (see Jaisson and Rosenbaum (2016); Gnabeyeu et al. (2026)).

such processes are used to mimic Fractional Brownian motion-driven stochastic differential equations

path theory or regularity structures.

1.2
Our contribution
The aim of this paper is to study, within a general framework, stochastic Volterra equations (1.1)
with path-dependent coefficients. We focus in particular on the case where the kernel may be either
singular or non-singular. Let ŌÜ be a deterministic function and let X0 be an H-valued random variable.
Throughout this paper, we allow the initial input x0(t) := X0ŌÜ(t) to be an F0-measurable random
function. This choice does not introduce any additional technical difficulty. A more subtle difficulty
arises from existing results on the integrability and pathwise regularity of solutions to Volterra equa-
tions (1.1). Indeed, following Ruan (2020), existence and uniqueness of a pathwise continuous and
integrable solution are established only under rather stringent assumptions.
(i) Our first objective is therefore to establish existence and uniqueness in Theorem 2.7 of a strong
solution to (1.1) on appropriate functional space when X0 ‚ąąLp(P) for any p large enough.
In contrast to the techniques developed for standard Stochastic Volterra equations in Zhang (2010)
and further refined in Jourdain and Pag`es (2022), our approach in Theorem 2.7 is based on a fixed-
point argument in appropriate Bochner spaces under additional appropriate integrability and regularity
conditions on the kernels.
(ii) We also analyze various qualitative properties of these solutions in Theorem 2.8, in particular,
the existence of continuous modification, their path regularity, supremum-norm estimates and
finite Lp moments, all of which are recovered a posteriori via Kolmogorov‚Äôs continuity criterion
and, then, the flow properties of the solution.
In Theorem 2.10, we show that our results can be extended to the more natural setting in which X0
belong to Lp(P) for some p ‚ąą(0, +‚ąě), by proving a representation formula for the solutions to (1.1)
as functionals of the Brownian path and the initial value.
(iii) Finally, we introduce and analyze an interpolated K-integrated Euler scheme, a key tool that
gives a clear road-map for the numerical simulation of those equations (1.1).
We prove its
convergence towards the unique strong solution, and derive the rate of this convergence in Lp.
Specifically, we prove in Theorem (3.8) that, if X0 ‚ąąLp(P) for some p > 0, then the scheme converges
in Lp(P) for the sup-norm, under the same assumptions (up to time regularity of the coefficients of the
equation) as those ensuring strong existence and uniqueness for the path-dependent Volterra process.
1.3
Plan of the paper and Notations
Organization of this paper. The remainder of the paper is organized as follows. Section 2 presents
the setting and the main results, including the existence and uniqueness theorem for path-dependent
3
stochastic Volterra integral equations with Lipschitz continuous drift and diffusion coefficients in the
Bochner space. Section 3 then focuses on the convergence of a discretization scheme to this process,
namely the interpolated K-integrated Euler-Maruyama scheme, as the time step tends to 0. Rates
of convergence are presented, together with insights into the performance of the scheme in path-
dependent settings. In Section 3, we numerically illustrate sample paths of a scaled process and verify
the convergence rate when K is the fractional kernel. Finally, in Section 5, we provide the proof of the
main results of this paper.
