Our Contributions.
In this paper, we introduce a new approach to bound eigenvalue
perturbation in both symmetric and non-symmetric settings.
Our results‚ÄĒTheorem 2.1,
Corollaries 2.2‚Äď2.4 in the symmetric case, and Theorem 7.2 in the non-symmetric case, show
that under a mild assumption, the least singular value of A and ňúA are of the same order of
magnitude. This means that the condition number of the matrix does not get worse under
noise. Our new results complement (3) because our underlying assumption becomes easier
to satisfy, and the result becomes more efficient, as ‚ą•A‚ą•increases (or the noise goes to zero,
as in the model A + ŌĶE, ŌĶ ‚Üí0).
Our strategy is based on the notion of regional stability of eigenvalues, which we introduce
in Section 3.1. This notion enables us to reduce the bounding of eigenvalue perturbation
to the bounding of eigenspace perturbation. We can use our framework to bound the per-
turbation of any eigenvalue or singular value, not only the smallest one. For example, we
also obtain new results for the perturbation of the leading eigenvalues, Corollaries 3.5-3.7.
These results extend well-known results in random matrix theory concerning the outliers in
deformed Wigner matrices.
Our method works for the deterministic setting, and we will discuss it in detail in Section 3.
In order to bound the perturbation of eigenspaces, we find a new contour analytic argument,
with which we prove a new, improved version of the classical Davis-Kahan bound regarding
perturbation of eigenspaces (Theorem 4.1). This argument refines several earlier approaches
(Subsection 4.1) and could be of independent interest as well.
