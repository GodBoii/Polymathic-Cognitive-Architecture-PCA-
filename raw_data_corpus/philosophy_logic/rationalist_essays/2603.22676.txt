RANDOM PERTURBATIONS OF SYSTEMS WITH PERIODIC IMPULSE EFFECTS
ASHIF KHAN AND CHETAN D. PAHLAJANI
Department of Mathematics, Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar, Palaj, Gandhinagar 382055, India
Abstract. The principal aim of the present work is to explore limit theorems for small random perturba-
tions of dynamical systems with periodic impulse effects, in the limit of vanishing noise intensity. We start
with a system whose time evolution is governed by a nonlinear ordinary differential equation in between
impulses, and a nonlinear resetting map at impulses; the latter are assumed to arrive in a time-periodic
manner. We next consider small state-dependent Brownian perturbations of this system and explore the
zero noise limit on finite, but arbitrary, time horizons. For the resulting stochastic system with impulse
effects, we prove convergence to the underlying deterministic impulsive system as the noise goes to zero.
More importantly, we prove convergence of the rescaled fluctuation process about the deterministic limit
in a strong pathwise sense on finite time intervals to a limiting fluctuation process governed by a linear
time-dependent stochastic differential equation in between impulses and a linear time-dependent resetting
map at impulses. The results are illustrated numerically for a periodically kicked nonlinear pendulum with
state-dependent kick sizes.
1. Introduction
Several problems in engineering and the natural sciences can be formulated in terms of systems with
impulse effects (sie), where the continuous evolution of the state according to an ordinary differential equa-
tion (ode) is punctuated by instantaneous jumps or impulses governed by suitable resetting maps, with the
impulses occuring at discrete times. The impulses may model impacts in mechanical systems [Bro16] such
as walking robots [GAP01] or percussive drilling [DDD13], but may also serve as convenient abstractions in
situations where a control action or forcing function is applied in extremely short intense bursts. Examples
of this latter category arise in impulsive control for robotic or spacecraft systems [KM20], [KMK21], [JCS21],
kicked oscillators [WY03], [LY10], [Moe14], neuroscience [Lin06], [LSB11], among many others.
Many realistic systems are better modeled by also incorporating in the dynamics a stochastic perturbation,
frequently taken to be a Brownian motion; this may reflect fluctuating external forces or unmodelled aspects
of the true dynamics. The state of the system is now given by a stochastic process which solves a stochastic
differential equation (sde) driven by Brownian motion [KS91], [Oks13]. Since the random perturbations in
question are typically small, the asymptotic behavior of these sde in the limit of vanishing noise intensity is
of great interest. One line of inquiry is to explore the zero noise limit of the stochastic process, and to also
characterize the limiting (rescaled) fluctuations of the process about this limit; these are questions in the
spirit of the Law of Large Numbers and the Central Limit Theorem. Also of great interest are results on large
deviations [DZ98], [FW12], which quantify the exponential rate of decay for probabilities of rare events in
the zero noise limit. These asymptotic questions have been extensively investigated for randomly perturbed
smooth systems, and also to some extent in nonsmooth settings; see, for instance, [BOQ09], [JS14], [HZG22]
and the references therein. However, the case of sie appears to not yet have been considered.
In the present work, we consider an sie whose state vector x(t) ∈Rd evolves according to a smooth vector
field b : Rd →Rd in between impulses, with a smooth resetting map x 7→h(x) governing the dynamics at
impulses, the latter arriving in a time-periodic manner; such systems arise when studying the dynamics of
ode forced by a periodic train of delta functions (Remark 2.9). Subjecting the continuous (ode) dynamics
to small Brownian perturbations of size ε ∈(0, 1) with state-dependent diffusion matrix σ, we study the

To better situate our work within the context of the existing literature, we start by noting that sie form
a class of nonsmooth dynamical systems [Fil88], [dBBCK08] with hybrid dynamics; by the latter we mean
a system whose evolution involves the close interplay between continuous and discrete dynamics [GST12].
Within sie, one can distinguish between problems with time-dependent versus state-dependent impulses.
For the first of these categories, the impulse times are known a priori, or at any rate, are independent of
the state. This includes, in addition to the present work, [WY03], [LY10], [DDD13], [Bal16], [JCS21] where
the impulses, whether originating due to kicks or control action, arrive in a time-periodic manner. The
second category includes cases where impulses, whether due to impact or control action, occur when the
state hits a suitable switching manifold, e.g., [GAP01], [VRP19], [KM20], [KMK21], [LHJ25]. For systems
with impacts/impulse effects subject to stochastic perturbations, questions of averaging, bifurcations, steady-
state behavior have also been explored; see, for instance, [DI04], [RG20], [NP05], [Zhu15], and the references
therein.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. In Section 2, we precisely formulate our problem of interest
and state our main results, viz., Theorems 2.5 and 2.6. Section 3 is devoted to the proof of Theorem 2.5,
while Section 4 tackles the proof of Theorem 2.6. Finally, in Section 5, we numerically illustrate our results
on a simple example: the periodically kicked nonlinear pendulum with nonlinear resetting map.

˙x1 = x2,

n=1, then
with x = (x1, x2) ∈R2, the system described in (45), (46) matches (3) with b(x) = (x2, −α sin x1)⊤,
