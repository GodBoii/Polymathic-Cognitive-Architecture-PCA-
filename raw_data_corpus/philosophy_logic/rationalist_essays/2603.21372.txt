POLYNOMIALS IN c-FREE RANDOM VARIABLES WITH
APPLICATIONS TO FREE DENOISING
ADRI´AN CELESTINO, FRANZ LEHNER, AND KAMIL SZPOJANKOWSKI
Abstract. We study distributions of polynomials in conditionally free (c-free) random
variables, a notion of independence for two-state noncommutative probability spaces
(A, φ, ψ) introduced by Bo˙zejko, Leinert and Speicher. To this end we establish recursive
relations between the joint Boolean cumulants of c-free random variables, analogous
to previously found recursions for Boolean cumulants of free random variables.
The
algebraic reformulation of these recursions on the free associative algebra provides an
effective formal machinery for the computation of the moment generating functions and
thus the distributions of arbitrary self-adjoint polynomials in c-free random variables.
As an application of a recent observation, our approach can be used to determine
conditional expectations of the form E a|P(a, b), where P is a self-adjoint polynomial
in free (in the sense of Voiculescu) random variables a, b. We illustrate this with an
example where P(a, b) “ i[a, b].
Finally we define orthogonal projections that formally play the role of conditional
expectations in the framework of c-freeness and share some properties with the con-
ditional expectations of free variables. In particular they can be used to re-derive by
purely algebraic methods the formula of Popa and Wang for the Σ-transform for the
c-free multiplicative convolution.

1. Introduction
The notion of c-freeness (or conditional freeness) was introduced by Bo˙zejko, Leinert,
and Speicher in [10, 9]. It is a noncommutative independence in the framework of a two-
state noncommutative probability space (M, φ, ψ). This concept generalizes freeness in
the following sense: random variables a, b P M are c-free with respect to the pair (φ, ψ),
if they are free in the sense of Voiculescu with respect to the second state ψ and satisfy
a factorization condition with respect to the first state φ. For precise definitions of both
types of independence see Section 2 below.
The distribution of a self-adjoint element a in a two-state probability space is described
by a pair of measures µφ
a and µψ
a , corresponding to the distributions with respect to φ
and ψ, respectively. In this paper, we present a method for determining the distribution
of polynomials in c-free variables. More precisely, for c-free variables a, b and a noncom-
mutative polynomial P P C⟨X, Y ⟩, we provide a method for determining the (ordinary)
moment generating function of P(a, b) with respect to φ (see Theorem 4.16). For simplic-
ity, our presentation focuses on the case of two variables, but this generalizes easily to the
case of n variables. Our main tools are the same as in [18] where we studied polynomials
in free variables:
‚ derivations on the free algebra which translate combinatorial recursions for Boolean
cumulants into effective algebraic identities between their generating functions;
‚ linearization, which allows us to express the resolvent (1 ´ zP(X, Y ))´1 in the
form ut(I ´ zAX ´ zBY )´1v for vectors u, v P Cn and n ˆ n matrices A and B.
On the combinatorial side of this development we prove a new characterization of c-
freeness in terms of Boolean cumulants (see Theorem 3.5). It turns out to be very similar
to the characterization of freeness from [12], and involves the so-called Vertical-No-Repeat
property which identifies maximal elements among coloured noncrossing partitions with
respect to a partial order denoted by !, which arises naturally in the context of free
probability (see [6]).
Our motivation to study this problem comes from the recent developments in [13] where
a surprising connection between c-freeness and so called free denoising was established,
which we briefly describe below. Let (M, ψ) be a tracial W ˚-probability space, i.e., M
is a von Neumann algebra with a faithful, normal trace ψ. Assume further that two
random variables a, b are free with respect to ψ. We interpret the random variable a as
a “signal” of interest and b as a “noise”, and we view P(a, b) as a noisy version of a,
where P P C⟨X, Y ⟩. We are interested in the conditional expectation E[a|P(a, b)], which
is the best approximation of a by a function of P(a, b) with respect to the L2-norm with
respect to ψ. It was shown in [13] that, under the assumptions a ě 0 and ψ(a) “ 1, if
we define a new state φ(c) “ ψ(ac) then a, b are c-free with respect to the pair (φ, ψ),
and moreover we have E[a|P(a, b)] “ h(P(a, b)) where h “ dµφ

This paper is organized as follows. In Section 2 we recall basic definitions and facts
concerning freeness and c-freeness. In Section 3 we prove a characterization of c-freeness in
terms of Boolean cumulants. In Section 4 we provide a method of computing distributions
of polynomials in c-free variables. In Section 5 we present an application of our result to
a free denoising problem. In Section 6 we study right quasi-conditional expectations and
present a recursion for them based on Boolean cumulants. In Section 7 we derive the Σ-
transform for c-free multiplicative convolution using right quasi-conditional expectations.

4. Distribution of polynomials in c-free variables
In this section, we derive a method for determining the distribution of polynomials in
c-free variables. Our main tool is a kind of differential calculus on the algebra of non-
commutative polynomials, and more generally on formal power series whose coefficients
are noncommutative polynomials. This framework is essential for analyzing distributions
of c-free variables. In a general noncommutative probability space, elements may satisfy
nontrivial relations (e.g., a2 “ 1) that invalidate the Leibniz rule of the derivations defined
below. No such obstructions arise in the algebra of noncommutative polynomials, making
this setting more suitable for our purposes.
4.1. Noncommutative polynomials and formal power series.

6. Algebraic conditional expectations of c-free variables
6.1. Conditional expectations. In this section, we study formal analogues of condi-
tional expectations for c-free random variables. It turns out that after some modifications
the algebraic machinery from [18] still works in the c-free setting, and we obtain projec-
tions onto subalgebras which formally act like conditional expectations, but lack important
analytic properties like positivity. Yet they are useful for practical computations and as
an example in Section 7 we recompute the c-free analog of the Σ-transform from [22] by
purely algebraic means.
Recall that a conditional expectation is a state-preserving projection EA : M Ñ A from
a noncommutative probability space M onto a subalgebra A such that in addition for
every u P M and every a1, a2 P A
φ(a1u) “ φ(a1EA[u]),
φ(ua2) “ φ(EA[u]a2).
(6.1)
In general such a map does not necessarily exist. A sufficient condition is that M is
a finite von Neumann algebra and φ is tracial, i.e. φ(ab) “ φ(ba) for all a, b P M [26,
Proposition 5.2.36]; for a characterization in the general case see [25]. If it does exist and
the state φ is faithful, then the conditional expectation EA[u] is uniquely determined by
the invariance property (6.1) and it follows that EA : M Ñ A is a unital completely
positive A-bimodule map, i.e.,
(6.2)
EA[a1ua2] “ a1EA[u]a2
for all u P M and a1, a2 P A.
A note on notation: The standard notation for the conditional expectation onto a
subalgebra A is EA and when the subalgebra A is generated by a single element a we also
write Ea Occasionally we write the latter also as E[x|a] “ Ea[x], for example in Section 5
in order to avoid clumsy expressions like Ei[a,b].
6.2. Left and right quasi-conditional expectations. From a technical point of view,
the computation of the conditional expectation of an element u P M onto a subalge-
bra A Ď M boils down to the construction of an element EA[u] P A which satisfies
