MATRIX LIBERATION PROCESS III:
UNITARY BROWNIAN MOTION AND MARTINGALE ANALYSIS
YOSHIMICHI UEDA
Abstract. We investigate the rate functions that emerge in our previous works towards large devia-
tion principle for the matrix liberation process driven by the unitary Brownian motion as well as the
unitary Brownian motion itself. Our approach is grounded in the viewpoint of the martingale problem.
Specifically, we formulate and solve a ‚Äúfree martingale problem‚ÄĚ within this framework, which provides
a new perspective on the underlying stochastic structure.
1. Introduction
This work is a sequel to our previous papers [29, 30] on the matrix liberation process, which we
introduced as a natural random matrix model of the liberation process introduced by Voiculescu [33].
Specifically, in the present paper, we investigate the rate functions that emerged in our prior studies
toward a large deviation principle for the matrix liberation process as well as for the unitary Brownian
motion, primarily from the perspective of martingale analysis. Furthermore, we establish a weak large
deviation lower bound for these processes.
In Part VI [33] of his series of free entropy, Voiculescu introduced and developed the free mutual
information i‚ąó, which plays the rňÜole of mutual information in free probability. Defined for tuples of sub-
algebras in a tracial W ‚ąó-probability space, i‚ąówas constructed via the liberation process in the same spirit
as the microstate-free free entropy (see [32]). A decade later, the orbital free entropy Ōáorb was introduced
by Hiai, Miyamoto and us [16] under certain constraints, following the same spirit as microstates free
entropy (see [31]), and was then generalized to full generality by us [27] (see also [4]).
Since the negative orbital free entropy ‚ąíŌáorb shares many properties with the free mutual information
i‚ąó, we conjecture that the orbital free entropy is a microstates counterpart of free mutual information. This
naturally leads to the unification problem on free mutual information, asking whether these two quantities
are proportional. This question is the mutual information equivalent of the celebrated unification problem
on free entropy (see e.g., [34]).
The unification problem on free entropy was solved completely in the one-dimensional case by Voiculescu
[32] using ‚Äúcommutative analysis‚ÄĚ. In the multi-dimensional setting, it was partially solved as a byprod-
uct of the large deviation upper bound established by Biane‚ÄďCapitaine‚ÄďGuionnet [3] for matrix Brownian
motion via ‚Äúfree stochastic analysis‚ÄĚ. By contrast, the unification problem on free mutual information
has thus far been solved only for the case of two projections (see [17, 9, 18, 15, 14]), results which are
intimately connected to the free Jacobi process introduced by Demni [12].
In this series of works, we have focused on the methodology of Biane‚ÄďCapitaine‚ÄďGuionnet [3], which
is centered on the level 3 large deviation principle. Although an important consequence in their work
regarding free entropy was recently reproved by Jekel‚ÄďPi [21] using a more elementary alternative method,
we maintain our focus on the approach of [3]. Our objective is twofold: not only to address the unification
problem on free mutual information but also to further advance the field of ‚Äúfree stochastic analysis‚ÄĚ as
a byproduct.

Solving our martingale problem requires developing a kind of stochastic calculus based on non-
commutative martingales. Recently, such a general theory focusing on free probability has been developed
by Jekel‚ÄďKemp‚ÄďNikitopoulos [20]. Their work partially motivated the present study and clarified the na-
ture of the problem; however, their theory is too broad to be applied directly to our concrete problem.
Thus, we must develop a more ‚Äúspecific theory‚ÄĚ tailored to our particular non-commutative martingales.
This is carried out in the present paper by building upon existing results from Pisier‚ÄďXu‚Äôs seminal work
[25], as well as an important work by Dabrowski [10] that refined the L¬īevy-type characterization of free
Brownian motion mentioned earlier.
With the solution to our free martingale problem explained above, we will prove, following the pro-
cedure in [3], a large deviation lower bound for the n-dimensional (left-increment) Brownian motion on
the unitary group U(N) with speed N2. By the standard contraction principle for the large deviation
principle (see e.g., [11]), this result also provides, in principle, a large deviation lower bound for the
matrix liberation process as well. Furthermore, we establish the almost sure convergence of the empirical
distribution of an n-dimensional process on the unitary group U(N), defined by a stochastic differential
equation (SDE) in the usual sense, which is analogous to (1.2) with a special kind of drift.
As mentioned above, we will mainly work within a framework that captures the (free) unitary Brownian
motion rather than the (matrix) liberation process. To this end, we will recall the content of Section 7
of [30] in some detail in Section 2. Section 3 provides an alternative representation of the rate function.
In Section 4 we solve the free martingale problem mentioned above and apply it to Brownian motion
on U(N), including a large deviation lower bound for it in Section 5. In Section 6 we apply the results
obtained to the matrix liberation process.
Finally, Appendix A provides a brief explanation of non-
commutative L2-spaces and affiliated operators, using formulations and notations that fit ‚Äúfree stochastic
analysis‚ÄĚ.
