Abstract
We consider the problem of Online Convex Optimization (OCO) with two-point bandit
feedback in an adversarial environment. In this setting, a player attempts to minimize a
sequence of adversarially generated convex loss functions, while only observing the value
of each function at two points.
While it is well-known that two-point feedback allows
for gradient estimation, achieving tight high-probability regret bounds for strongly convex
functions still remained open as highlighted by Agarwal et al. (2010). The primary chal-
lenge lies in the heavy-tailed nature of bandit gradient estimators, which makes standard
concentration analysis difficult. In this paper, we resolve this open challenge by providing
the first high-probability regret bound of O(d(log T + log(1/őī))/¬Ķ) for ¬Ķ-strongly convex
losses. Our result is minimax optimal with respect to both the time horizon T and the
dimension d.

While full-information OCO is well-studied, the bandit feedback setting, where only the
values of ‚Ąďt are observed, remains a central challenge due to the lack of direct gradient
information.
To bridge the gap between bandit and full-information feedback where the gradient
information of the loss function ‚Ąďt(¬∑) can be obtained, Agarwal et al. (2010) introduced the
multi-point bandit setting. In their seminal work, they demonstrated that evaluating the
loss function at two randomized points allows the player to construct gradient estimators
that mimic the behavior of true gradients. However, as explicitly discussed in their paper,
achieving tight high-probability regret bounds for strongly convex loss functions under two-
point feedback remained an unresolved challenge. Specifically, while expectation bounds
were established, the heavy-tailed nature of bandit estimators against an adversary has
historically made high-confidence guarantees elusive.
In this work, we resolve this open problem by providing the first high-probability re-
gret bound for two-point feedback OCO with ¬Ķ-strongly convex losses. Our main result

Distinct from the conventional approach that directly reduces the analysis to smoothed
loss functions under standard Online Gradient Descent (OGD), we propose a novel analyt-
ical framework. By departing from this reduction-based paradigm, we are able to derive a
bound that is more robust to the variance of the zero-order estimator.
Furthermore, our result significantly improves the dependency on the dimension d. We
reduce the regret from the O(d2) factor found in prior work (Agarwal et al., 2010). This
improvement is achieved by extending the sophisticated geometric and probabilistic tech-
niques introduced by Shamir (2017), which were used to prove a regret holding in expec-
tation, to the high-probability regime. By generalizing these techniques to accommodate
high-confidence concentration, we match the information-theoretic lower bounds for this
online convex optimization setting.

4 Conclusion
In this paper, we have addressed a fundamental gap in the theory of bandit online convex
optimization by providing the first tight high-probability regret bound for strongly con-
vex functions with two-point feedback. By resolving the open problem posed by Agarwal
et al. (2010), we have shown that it is possible to achieve a regret bound of O(d(log T +
log(1/őī))/¬Ķ), which is optimal in terms of all key parameters.
Our technical contribution lies in a departure from the standard reduction-based anal-
ysis. By developing a direct analytical framework and generalizing the multi-point feed-
back probabilistic techniques to the high-confidence regime, we have successfully reduced
the dimensional dependency from O(d2) to O(d).
This result bridges the gap between
expectation-based bounds and high-probability guarantees in online convex optimization
settings.
